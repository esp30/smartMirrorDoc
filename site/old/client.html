What are we trying to solve?

We are consuming ever bigger portions on ever larger plates!

If you want to see how inflated our portion sizes have become, don’t go to the supermarket – head to an antique shop. You spot a tiny goblet clearly designed for a doll, only to be told it is a “wine glass”. What look like side plates turn out to be dinner plates. The real side plates resemble saucers.

Our gigantic problem with portions: why are we all eating too much?, The Guardian

Why should we care about the size of our portions?

Research suggests that people with more food in front of them tend to eat more, whether it's served to them on plates or they serve themselves from a container.
But few of us are actually doing anything to make up for it. At no other period in history have we faced the problem of too much food instead of too little.

In canteens the problem is worse

In self-serving canteens and refectories, the challenge of assessing the correct portions size is amplified since the contents/ingredients of each recipe are ignored by the public. For the canteen workers, the main challenge is the difficulty in adapting adequate food portions for each person profile, needs and goals.

How are we trying to solve the problem?

By creating a system that allows real-time advisory to the users of how much they can eat given their person profile and needs (such as the diet and food restrictions), allowing for a better portion control and diversity in their plate.

What will you need to implement our solution?

We are creating a system to solve the portions problem using weight and proximity sensors, several Raspberry Pi 3 to control the several Food Stands in the Canteen and to receive and send information into a central server that can be implemented using any PC. More details in System Overview.

What have we done?

  • For Demo 1, we proved that the project is doable by integrating several components and implementing a few key features (see Features). After 15 May (Demo 2), more info will be provided about what we have done.
  • For Demo 2 (Milestone 4), we've implemented the final Database, and all the modules that interact directly with the users, specifically the UI on the Food Stands Displays.
